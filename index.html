Huawei Technologies India Private Limited in association with ACM India is organizing 2 day online technical research summit on 27th - 28th Oct. The main theme of this event is advancements and future of large scale multi modal data engineering in AI/ML era.

This two-day tech research summit will bring together technology leaders, innovators, entrepreneurs & thought provokers who are passionate about addressing impactful problems by turning ideas into solutions.
The tech event is comprised of 4 tracks which has following focus areas. Each track consists of invited talks and panel discussion.

Focus Areas

Each track will focus on a specific area with invited talks and a panel discussion.

1
Latest research and industry challenges/cases sharing on areas of low resource language NLP, NLU, ranking, KG, recommender systems

Proliferation of deep learning techniques and availability of efficient computing infrastructure has allowed research community to make great strides in the areas like NLP, NLU, recommender systems to name a few. The various advancements achieved in the niche areas of

  • NLP, NLU for low resource languages, transfer learning, machine translation, multilingual aspects
  • Knowledge graphs – extraction, construction, refinement
  • Algorithms related to context aware personalized recommendation systems
2
Advancements in large scale multilingual models, deployments challenges, intelligent operations and site reliability engineering
  • Creative generation using GAN and multi-modal data fusion
  • Multi modal content understanding, correlation and summarization using NLP and computer vision
  • Latest trends in deployment of the large-scale multi-lingual models, recommender systems and fusion data models.
  • Intelligent operations advancement & site reliability engineering (autonomous fault diagnosis & self-healing, autonomous decision making in a highly dynamic cloud environment)
3
Advancement in multimedia frameworks/pipelines for consumer devices

Increase in the consumer devices and IOT devices with new sensors, multimedia processing intelligently has become an important aspect of consumer domain.

  • Multimedia frameworks/pipelines & it’s future direction, industry adoption strategy, challenges etc (Gstreamer, Piperwire, PulseAudio, StageFright, ffmpeg , Linux foundation NN Streamer, Intel OpenVINO, RidgeRunner, NVidia Jarvis etc)
  • Enhancement of multimedia frameworks with AI
  • IOT world through AI
  • Multimodal, multimedia handling at edge devices
4
How the modern computer systems and systems-AI are answering the grand challenge problems of data store

Modern computer systems have enabled the AI/ML systems that are compute and data intensive. However the rising demand also brings new challenges and opportunities, some of which are:

  • Lack of memory bandwidth to move data from memory to CPU leading to latency. DIMM capacity is stagnant but the SSD capacity is increasing. These are major choke point for scale and performance for Big data workload.
  • Computational storage is aiding to scale the memory and compute, new ways of achieving scalability. Compute offloading to GPU, SmartNIC in the database (Join)/AI(graph) scenario.
  • OS virtualization, containerization have come up as a new way of computing in the era of cloud computing. The system challenges of scale, performance, reliability.
  • How do we make systems level decisions more robust with the aid of AI/ML for fault prediction, capacity/mem-bandwidth prediction, caching, compression etc.

KITHCT Chair Professor, Director at NUS-Tsinghua Extreme Search Center (NExT), National University of Singapore

Abstract:

In this research, we focus on extending the research from visual object recognition to that of relation inference. It permits the system to extract basic knowledge relation triplets in the form of which elevates the extractable semantic of video to that of text, and supports a wide range of higher level video-text fusion tasks such as the multi-perspective video captioning, video QA, and multimodal knowledge graph. It also supports causal reasoning, such as the counterfactual inference. In this talk, he will present the background on object recognition, and present his recent works in transforming the research from object recognition to video relation inference and causal reasoning. He will conclude the talk by outlining several future research directions and applications. As part of this research, we have constructed and released a large-scale video dataset to facilitate relevant research in this emerging field. The dataset has been used for a grand challenge in the ACM Multimedia 2019 conference.

Abstract:

As artificial intelligence (AI) begins to impact various aspects of our lives, there is a need to combine it with a better understanding of drivers of human behavior. We describe our ongoing work in diverse areas, including health and information access where combining AI with human factors is key to developing an effective solution. We present an AI-based approach to modeling the diligence of health workers in a public health scenario, which is needed to ensure that data being fed to downstream algorithms is of high quality. We describe additional scenarios of "data corruption" in the context of information access where the "corruption" of data is not a bug, but a feature based on natural human behavior. We then present a wellness-based approach to improve health outcomes for people, which requires a combination of mobile sensing, machine learning and influencing human behavior. Finally, we describe a deep learning based modeling of human behavior in a specific game-based setting, which has very interesting implications if we are able to generalize that approach to broader settings.

Abstract:

Information Extraction is the task of identifying argument-relation-argument tuples from sentences. Traditional IE approaches use a pre-defined relation vocabulary, whereas Open IE outputs textual relations without a given ontology. In this talk, I describe our recent results on neural models for both Open IE, as well as distantly supervised relation extraction. We also describe automatic data collection approaches to extend monolingual (English) IE approaches to multilingual IE.

Dr. Dilip K. Prasad is currently an associate professor at Department of Computer Science, UiT The Arctic University of Norway. He completed PhD from Nanyang Technological University (NTU) in Singapore in 2013 and B.Tech in Computer Science from Indian Institute of Technology(ISM), Dhanbad in 2003. Abstract:

Computer vision for performing object segmentation in images and videos is relatively routine. Therefore, when a biologist asks an AI scientists if we can use AI for segmenting sub-cellular structures from optical microscopy images of living cells, one wonders what has been stopping this. The answer lies in the fact that for certain sub-cellular structures, the size of the structures and resolution of the microscopes are comparable. Therefore, the images contain an inaccurate depiction of the reality. Manual segmentation by experts who understand the morphologies of the sub-cellular structures as well as how microscopes distort the morphologies is the only way out. However, manual annotation for quantitative analysis in microscopy videos is a big bottleneck since it takes a few days to segment all mitochondria. We solve this problem by creating a simulated expert. The simulated expert simulates the diverse morphologies of the structures, the distortions introduced by an optical microscope, the target segmentation, as well as the AI model for segmentation. Through this simulation-supervised approach, we contribute a physic-based AI solution, which then reduces the large data annotation bottleneck from several man-months to a few seconds.

Institute Chair Professor, Department of CSE, IIT Bombay

Prof. Sunita Sarawagi is currently a Professor in Computer Science and Engineering Department at IIT Bombay, she is also the Professor In Charge of the Center of Machine Intelligence and Data Science at IIT-B. Her current research focuses current research interests are sequence models for text and time-series, domain adaptation, effective human intervention in learning, graphical models and structured learning. Abstract:

For various NLP tasks, multilingual language models (mLM) were designed to provide shared representations to enable transfer of task-specific supervision from high resource languages to Low web-resource languages (LRLs). However, incorporating a new language in an LM still remains a challenge, particularly for languages with limited corpora and in unseen scripts. We show how to learn representations for LRLs by bridging the gap between related languages using transliteration, dictionaries, and special alignment loss.

Another challenging low-resource setting is automatic speech recognition for accented speech. We show how to adapt blackbox ASR services for mainstream accents using a fine-grained splicing of the outputs of two models. We address the issue of collecting labeled data for new accents using an efficient data selection algorithm.

Abstract:

PipeWire is the new multimedia stream sharing system. It enables applications running on a device to share access to hardware devices, both audio and camera, but also to share streams with each other. It replaces PulseAudio and JACK on the audio side to provide low latency, low power audio service. It also enables screen sharing for Wayland compositors. PipeWire also provides a security barrier where the policy control is in a separate programmable demon.

In this talk, I'll introduce PipeWire and how it works. I'll also go over WirePlumber, the programmable policy service that makes it easy to create advanced policies specific to the device you're build.

Abstract:

Internet of Things (IoT) allows users to connect diverse kinds of devices to accumulate real-time data from the exclusive domain. The vast amount of complex data collected from the IoT domain is hard to process and analyze by utilizing regular tools. Further, the data in IoT are polymorphous, heterogeneous, large in quantity, and time-limited. There are also some difficulties in data transmitting as well as maintaining interoperability which necessitates the need for programming and statistical approaches for analyzing and managing the data. This talk will explore characteristics of data in IoT, various properties/ requirements for processing data in IoT driven technologies, and the intent to ascertain the emphases and difficulties of data processing in IoT. Abstract:

In this talk, we will take a closer look at Autonomous Vehicles and their various subsystems. We start with a bit of history and then dig into the various modules such as perception, localization, prediction, planning and control that enable Autonomous Driving. We will then address the enabling technologies that make a vehicle autonomous and how these technologies are integrated together to allow a vehicle to navigate streets autonomously. Next, we will discuss their current challenges and brainstorm how to overcome them. Abstract:

Graphs are de facto data structures for many data processing applications, and their volume is ever growing. Many graph processing tasks are computation intensive and/or memory intensive. Therefore, we have witnessed a significant amount of effort in accelerating graph processing tasks with heterogeneous architectures like GPUs, FPGAs and even ASIC. In this talk, we will first review the literatures of large graph processing systems on heterogeneous architectures.
